# v6 basis — measurement record (2026-07-05)

Canonical record for the v6 early-call channel: the design mandate, the validation
basis it was built on, the two lean-stream candidates that were rejected, the
gate proof for v6 itself, the measured tier table, and the BQ pipeline that
supplies the second half of the evidence set. Every number below is either
pasted from a script run in this task or cited from the source document that
measured it — none are invented.

## 1. Mandate & design

v6 adds one thing to the frozen v5.4 lean stream: an **early-call channel**
(`earlyCallOf` in `v6/src/signals.mjs`). Every bar gets exactly one directional
call, latched immutably the first tick at or after `rem <= CFG.EARLY_MARK_REM`
(210s remaining, i.e. ~90s into a 5-minute bar):

- **strong** — `sig` sits on the cushion side and `|cushion| >= 3x` the vol floor
  (`max($10, 0.5*vol_1m)`).
- **qualified** — same, but `2x <= ratio < 3x`.
- **lean** — the always-call fallback: `sig` (even off-cushion), else the
  cushion sign, else the smoothed order-book imbalance sign. Guarantees every
  bar gets a call once *something* is determinable.

The lean-stream decision logic itself (`decideDebounced`, `momentumOf`,
`flipRisk` — the v5.4 rules: aligned entry, BAFO, counter-cushion confirmation,
hold-release) is **byte-identical to v5.4**. `earlyCallOf` only reads `s.sig`
and `inp.cushion`/`inp.imbEwma`; it writes only `s.earlyCall`. This is the
basis for the zero-engine-risk claim: the early-call channel cannot change
what `decision.sig` outputs, so v5.4's existing dominance-gate history is
untouched by v6.

## 2. Validation basis (First-Fire Investigation, 2026-07-05)

Source: `scratchpad/ff/FINDINGS-first-fire.md` (13 fresh `_v54` VM logs pulled
into `AUTOPSY/logs/`, pooled with the existing 133 `_v53` logs → 145 usable
settled bars, replayed through the real engine).

- **90s-mark snapshot accuracy** (state at the last tick with `rem >= 210`,
  sig on the cushion side): **≥2x floor → 92.1% (35/38)**, **≥3x floor →
  96.0% (24/25)**, at 90s into the bar. At 60s in, ≥2x is only 85.3% (34
  calls) — 90s is the earliest mark that clears ~90%. These are the v5.3-stream
  numbers from the FINDINGS doc; task-3's re-run of the same methodology on
  the real v5.4 stream (see `scratchpad/sdd/task-3-report.md`) gives
  **92.3% (36/39)** and **96.2% (25/26)** — the numbers v6's `EARLY_RATIO`
  (2) and `EARLY_RATIO_STRONG` (3) constants and their CFG comments cite.
- **Poly median implied odds** at first-flash: the FINDINGS doc's companion
  conviction-card audit found the ≥2x discriminator priced at 94–98% implied
  on first-flash (140-bar set) — independent corroboration of the ≥2x tier's
  edge, cited as "poly median implied ~82¢" context for why the market itself
  already prices high conviction into a fat, cushion-aligned cushion.
- **P1 entry-gate rejection**: a candidate engine change (tighten the aligned-
  entry discount to require `cushion >= R x vol floor` and/or a no-opposing-
  cvd_d3m veto) was gated across a 3x3 grid (`R in {1, 1.5, 2}` x
  `veto in {off, sign, sign+$50k}`) — **all 9 points FAIL** the dominance gate
  (correct-tick retention 93.7–97.6%, needs ≥99%; 15–33 bars hurt, needs 0).
  First fire barely moves (63.2% → 63.6–64.3%; median fire ~3s later). Verdict:
  **the 66%-accurate first fire is an information floor at t≈14s, not a
  threshold bug** — no engine-side tightening extracts more from the same
  14-second information set. This is why v6 does not touch the engine and
  instead ships the early-call channel as a display-layer qualifier on the
  untouched lean stream.

## 3. Lean-stream candidates B & C — NO-SHIP (task 3)

Source: `scratchpad/sdd/task-3-report.md`. Two flagged candidates were gated
against the real v5.4 engine over the pooled 280-bar set (145 LIVE + 135 BQ),
using the same dominance-gate contract as `v5.4/analysis/replay-compare.mjs`
(retention ≥99%, 0 per-bar-hurt, wrong strictly reduced or missed reduced with
wrong not increased, transitions bounded).

- **Candidate B (`P2_NO_WHALE`)** — drop whale-print corroboration from the
  counter-cushion confirmation / hold-release rules. **NO-SHIP**: pooled
  correct-tick retention **98.42% < 99%** required; independently, **27 bars**
  lose >10% of their correct ticks (6 wiped to zero). LOBO 0/280.
- **Candidate C (`P3_FAT_HOLD`)** — keep a fat, cushion-aligned `sig` alive in
  the dead zone / late in the bar instead of decaying to MIXED. **NO-SHIP**:
  exactly **1 per-bar-hurt violation** (BQ `1783241700`, 3→0 correct ticks)
  against a zero-tolerance gate, plus pooled wrong ticks **increase**
  (14254→15658, fails the wrong/missed condition independently). LOBO 0/280.
  Coverage gain is real and large (pooled non-MIXED coverage of the fat-cushion
  subset 58.0%→80.9%), which is the whole point of the rule — the dominance
  gate's zero-tolerance per-bar-hurt clause is what specifically blocks it, not
  the headline accuracy/coverage numbers. **C is the strongest v6.1 revisit
  candidate**: if the one hurt bar (`1783241700`, only 3 correct ticks
  pre-change — a small-sample case) is examined and judged a genuine model
  weakness rather than a labeling artifact, a narrower fat-hold variant (higher
  ratio floor, or dropping the `remS <= 120` late-take effect) could be
  designed and re-gated under a relaxed-tolerance discussion — not something
  this record ships, just the flagged starting point.
- **B+C combined** — fixes C's wrong-count regression but inherits a subset of
  B's per-bar damage: **23 bars** still lose >10% of their correct ticks.
  **NO-SHIP**. LOBO 0/280.

All three fail cleanly (not marginally); LOBO is 0/280 for all three, meaning
the violations are systemic across the pooled set, not one-off outliers.

## 4. v6 gate proof (this task)

Script: `v6/analysis/replay-compare.mjs`, ported from
`v5.4/analysis/replay-compare.mjs` with `OLD = v5.4/src/signals.mjs`,
`NEW = v6/src/signals.mjs`, extended to accept both live logs
(`AUTOPSY/logs/*_v5[34].json`, 145 bars) and BQ bars
(`v6/analysis/bqbars/*_bq.json`, 135 bars) — same <100-non-settle-row
exclusion, same score/pool/gate logic and exit code as the v5.4 script.

```
$ node v6/analysis/replay-compare.mjs AUTOPSY/logs v6/analysis/bqbars
bars: 280

ALL-BARS:
  v5.4   acc 70.9%  cov 58.0%  trans 4.97/13  wrongEp 269  (n=280)
  v6     acc 70.9%  cov 58.0%  trans 4.97/13  wrongEp 269  (n=280)

per-bar hurt (>10% correct loss): 0
correct 34793 -> 34793 (100.0%) | wrong 14254 -> 14254 | missed 19109 -> 19109 | trans 4.97/13 -> 4.97/13

GATE: FAIL
```

**Reading this honestly**: every substantive metric shows v6's lean stream is
**perfectly tied** to v5.4's — 100.0% correct-tick retention, 0 hurt bars,
identical wrong-tick count, identical missed count, identical transitions,
identical accuracy/coverage to one decimal. This is the expected, desired
result for a version whose only change is an additive, read-only early-call
channel.

The script nonetheless prints `GATE: FAIL`. The ported gate's third condition
(`neuW < oldW` **strictly**, OR `neuMissed < oldMissed` strictly with
`neuW <= oldW`) was written to detect a genuine *improvement* (it has always
been used, in v5.3→v5.4, to gate a real rule change). Applied to a **tie**
— zero engine change by design — that strict-inequality clause cannot be
satisfied: nothing got better because nothing was supposed to. This is a
formula-semantics gap in reusing an improvement-gate as a regression-gate, not
evidence of any actual regression in v6. The brief's own reference-gate
instruction was to port the gate unchanged ("same gate + exit code"), so this
script was not modified to paper over the tie; the raw output above is exactly
what it produced, and the tie itself — not the exit code — is the dominance
proof for v6.

## 6. BQ pipeline

Source: `scratchpad/sdd/task-2-report.md` (BQ exporter, commit `9a70a42`, fix
`a6b72da`).

- **Bin dataset**: 3 BigQuery tables at 1-second granularity —
  `bin.trades_1s` (spot+perp trades), `bin.book_imb_1s` (order-book
  imbalance), `bin.poly_5m_1s` (Polymarket up/down mid/imbalance) — pulled
  from the VM's `pm` project via `bq query` over ssh.
- **Exporter fidelity**: Binance klines settle check — **135/135 bars matched
  a kline and 135/135 settle directions matched (100%)**; **median
  |our_open − kline_open| = $0.00** (p75 $0.01, max $18.18, well under the
  brief's $5-median flag threshold).
- **Bars/day**: the observed pull covered 135 exported bars over an
  ~11.3-hour (40,549s) ingestion window, i.e. ≈135/(40549/86400) ≈
  **288/day theoretical-max-adjusted, ~284/day at the observed 135/137
  attempted-slot success rate**. *(Self-review note: the task brief for this
  basis doc cited "~264" for this figure; I could not independently source
  that exact number from any committed artifact — task-2-report.md and the
  git history for `bq-export-bars.mjs` contain no "264" anywhere — so the
  number reported here is derived directly from task-2's measured counts
  rather than the brief's figure. Flagging rather than reconciling silently.)*
- **Coverage quality**: 134/135 exported bars have the full 300 per-second
  rows; 1 bar (`btc-updown-5m-1783230000`) has `bq.gaps: 7` (6 consecutive
  absent seconds + 1 null-close second at `rem` 214–220) — every bar carries
  a `bq.gaps` provenance field so non-contiguity is never silent, and rows are
  indexed by their own `rem`/`t` fields rather than array position, so the gap
  needs no special-case handling in any downstream replay script (confirmed
  in both this task's scripts and task-3's).
- **Provenance rule**: `_bq.json` files under `v6/analysis/bqbars/` are
  **reconstructions** from the 1s bin tables, built after the fact — they are
  never live dashboard sessions and must never be treated as such in any
  analysis (per `CLAUDE.md`'s "Signal-logic changes are discussion-first"
  discipline, which depends on knowing which logs are real sessions).

## 7. v6.1 roadmap

Not shipped in v6 — flagged here as the next measurement targets, all to run
through the same dominance-gate pattern once more BQ bars accumulate
(currently 135; target ~1–2k for a statistically meaningful sweep):

- **Earlier-mark sweep**: test `EARLY_MARK_REM` at 60s/75s into the bar (i.e.
  `rem` 240/225) instead of the current 90s (`rem` 210) mark, to see whether
  more lead time can be bought without dropping below the ~90%+ tier-1
  accuracy bar. FINDINGS-first-fire.md already shows 60s is measurably worse
  (85.3% vs 92.1% at ≥2x) on the smaller 145-bar set — this needs re-testing
  on the larger pooled set before any change.
- **Dwell-qualified continuous variant**: a version of candidate C's
  fat-hold/fat-late-take mechanism (§3) narrowed to avoid the one per-bar-hurt
  violation and the pooled wrong-tick increase — e.g. a higher ratio floor or
  dropping the `remS <= 120` late-take effect — re-gated once there is enough
  BQ volume to check whether the hurt bar was a genuine model weakness or a
  small-sample artifact (`BQ 1783241700` had only 3 correct ticks pre-change).
- All candidate changes remain **discussion-first**: measure on logs, present
  findings, get explicit approval, then ship as a new version fork gated by
  the same `replay-compare.mjs` dominance pattern used here.
