### The bar's true story, told by the one input that never lied

This was a razor-thin chop bar — settled DOWN by only $4.44 — and the cushion itself crossed sign **four times** (at rem 276, 190, 115, 71), splitting 313 negative ticks against 261 positive. The book (`ewma`) chopped with it, flipping sign across nearly every episode (+0.193, −0.018, −0.363, −0.226, +0.074, +0.094, −0.164, +0.332, −0.004…) and never settling on a consistent lean — that's the liar. What never wavered was flow and whale positioning: `cvd_since_open` went negative in the first tick (−69,537) and stayed negative the entire bar, bottoming at −476,494 and closing at −383,585; `large_prints` sat pinned at roughly −$123k of net whale selling for 430 of 590 ticks, never once flipping positive. `cvd_d3m` is the one exception worth flagging honestly — it started deeply negative (−737,609) and drained back toward flat by the close (−49,046), so the 3-minute flow window was directionally right but losing conviction as the bar wore on. Duplicate second-rows appear on exactly half the ticks (294 of 590) — a mechanical logging artifact, not a data signal, but it roughly doubles the raw tick counts inside the two WRONG UP episodes below.

### Why it was right when it was right

None of the four RIGHT episodes landed on a fat cushion — every one entered at a ratio between 0.28× and 0.92× the vol floor, squarely in the coin-flip zone this project has measured at 63–65%. Two of them (rem 152→128 and rem 64→60) are ordinary aligned DOWN calls that happened to land on the correct side of a thin, chopping cushion. The rem 211→161 episode is the standout: it's a counter-cushion entry against a positive cushion, corroborated only by whale prints — structurally the same shape as the `inverted-whale-corroborator` class this project has measured at 9–14% accurate — and here it worked. That's not evidence the class is safe; on a single bar it's a hit against odds that mostly run the other way, and it should be read as one data point, not a reversal of the measured base rate. The final DOWN (rem 1→1, 2 ticks) rode the flow-led momentum trigger into the close, matching the cushion that was already sitting at essentially the settle value.

### Why it was wrong when it was wrong — the pattern, precisely

Two of the three WRONG episodes carry the `thin-aligned-vs-flow` flag: rem 272→220 (cushion ratio 0.7×, `cvd_d3m` at −578,507) and rem 93→64 (cushion ratio 0.64×, `cvd_d3m` at −92,046). Both are the same mechanism this project has already documented — the aligned-entry discount drops the book threshold to 0.14 whenever the book agrees with cushion sign, but that discount doesn't check cushion magnitude or flow direction. A cushion this thin (well under 1× the vol floor) is treated the same as a fat, decisive one, and 3-minute flow running hundreds of thousands of dollars opposite gets no veto. BAFO doesn't rescue either tick: BAFO only fires *toward* a fat cushion (≥ max($30, 3× vol_1m)) when the book opposes it and both d60 and `cvd_d3m` agree with the cushion — but the cushion here never got fat (max magnitude all bar was $11.43, well under a plausible $30 fat threshold), so BAFO's precondition was never in play.

The third WRONG episode, rem 112→108 (flow-led, momentum firing UP from the dead zone at a 0.92× cushion), doesn't map to any flag in the script output or to a named class in the reference patterns — it's unclassified. It sits right at the edge of the thin/fat boundary (0.92×, closest to fat of any episode this bar) and momentum fired the call rather than book+cushion, which is a different trigger path than the aligned or counter-cushion mechanisms this project has characterized so far.

On the v5.4 mirror: 11 ticks diverge, and in both cases v5.4 goes silent (MIXED) rather than firing a new direction — it drops the wrong flow-led UP at rem 112→108 (all 9 diff ticks there) and it also drops the correct flow-led DOWN at rem 1 (2 diff ticks). Neither divergence is a BAFO firing — no new directional call appears in the mirror, only suppression of v5.3's flow-led calls — so whatever caused it isn't described by patterns.md's BAFO mechanism. Net effect on this bar: v5.4 trades away one correct call (2 ticks, essentially the last two seconds) to avoid one wrong call (9 ticks) — a wash in ticks, unclassified in mechanism.

**Candidate rules this autopsy supports** (queue, discussion-first, measured on the full set before touching anything): *thin-aligned flow veto* — this bar is fresh evidence for it: both flagged WRONG episodes are aligned entries at ratios (0.7×, 0.64×) below the vol floor with `cvd_d3m` running hundreds of thousands of dollars opposite, the same shape as the `1783188300` specimen already in the queue. No other candidate rule in the list maps to this bar's other failure (the unclassified flow-led UP at rem 112→108).
