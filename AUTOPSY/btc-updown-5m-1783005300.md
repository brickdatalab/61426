### Why it was right when it was right

Both correct calls (episodes 4 and 7) were aligned entries fired on thin cushions (0.27× and 0.29× the vol floor) — the 0.14 aligned discount let the engine call direction well before the cushion built real magnitude. They were not lucky: at entry, `ewma_entry` for both was already negative (-0.294 and -0.162), agreeing with the cushion sign, and both entries landed inside the stretch where `cvd_since_open`/`cvd_d3m` had already turned negative and stayed there through to settle. So the RIGHT episodes were thin-cushion calls that happened to have book and flow already lined up on the correct side — the discount worked here because nothing was fighting it.

Episode 6 carries the `thin-aligned-vs-flow` flag: a wrong aligned UP entry on a cushion sitting at exactly 1.0× the vol floor (the coin-flip boundary) while 3-minute flow (`cvd_d3m` entry -1176333, `cvd_since_open` entry -801706) was already running hard negative. The aligned 0.14 discount treats this thin cushion like a fat one and flow gets no veto — this class measures 63–65% at best for thin entries generally, and here the opposing flow made it worse.
