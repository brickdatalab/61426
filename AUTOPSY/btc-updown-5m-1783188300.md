### Why it was wrong when it was wrong — the pattern, precisely

All 104 wrong UP ticks share one signature, and it's *not* "fighting price" — every single UP tick had a positive cushion. The failure is: **the "aligned" entry (cushion sign + book ≥0.14) fired on wafer-thin cushions (+$3.29 both times!) while net CVD was massively, persistently negative — and the engine has no rule that lets flow veto a book+price entry.** The aligned-entry discount treats a +$3 cushion the same as a +$60 one. BAFO doesn't help because it's the mirror scenario: BAFO fires *with* flow when the *book* is the liar; here the book and a token cushion agreed with each other while the *flow* was the truth-teller, and flow had no vote.

This is the same tier we just indicted in the conviction analysis: thin-cushion states run 63–65% everywhere we measure them. Here's a bar where both wrong episodes entered at cushion ratios far below 1× the vol floor with 3-minute flow opposite by hundreds of thousands of dollars.

**Candidate rule this autopsy suggests** (for the queue, discussion-first, needs the 140-bar measurement before anyone touches the engine): *thin-cushion aligned entries require flow non-opposition* — e.g., the 0.14 aligned discount only applies when `|cushion| ≥ the vol floor` OR `cvd_since_open`/`d3m` isn't strongly opposite; otherwise the entry needs the full 0.20.
