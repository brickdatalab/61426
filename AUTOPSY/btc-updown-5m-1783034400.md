### Why it was wrong when it was wrong — the pattern, precisely

All four wrong episodes (2, 4, 7, 9) carry the same flag: **`thin-aligned-vs-flow`** — an aligned DOWN entry on a cushion well under 1× the vol floor (0.48×, 0.47×, 0.42×, 0.19×) while 3-minute flow ran strongly opposite, with no veto available to flow. This bar makes the mechanism unusually stark: `cvd_d3m`'s floor for the *entire session* was +68,246 — it was never negative, not even during episodes 2 and 4 early in the bar — yet the aligned 0.14 discount let a −$2.71-to−$6 cushion fire DOWN four separate times against that same persistently positive flow. The aligned discount treats a few-dollar cushion the same as a $60 one, and flow has no vote once the discount clears. This is the class measured 63–65% accurate in the thin-cushion tier generally, and here it went 0-for-4.

BAFO doesn't rescue any of these because its precondition never held: BAFO only fires *toward* a fat cushion when the *book* is the dissenting voice and both d60 and cvd_d3m agree with the cushion. In all four wrong episodes the book agreed with the (thin) DOWN cushion — there was no book-vs-cushion disagreement for BAFO to act on, and the cushion itself never reached the fat threshold on those entries anyway.

The 15-tick v5.4 divergence sits entirely inside episode 13 (rem 27→20): v5.3 flow-led into UP immediately, v5.4 held MIXED for those same ticks before agreeing. Both engines land on the same final call and the same RIGHT verdict for episode 13 — this is v5.4's flow-led gate being stricter/slower to trigger, costing lead time but not costing the outcome on this bar.
