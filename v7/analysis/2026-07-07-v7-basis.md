# v7 basis — measurement record (2026-07-07)

Canonical record for the v7 engine generation (`v7s/` standard + `v7c/` conviction): the
mission, the measurements that shaped the design, the out-of-sample reversal, the ship
record, and the queued direction. Written from the full build-session transcript
(sesn_011EPHf9PGFiXfigPHrgePT9, 2026-07-07). Companion condensed doc: `v7/README.md`.

## 1. Mission & spec

Goal as given: a new plug-and-play engine version where **the fired signal must equal the
settled direction**, firing **within the first 1–2 minutes** of any BTC 5m bar, at the
highest achievable conviction. Chosen operating point (user decisions, 2026-07-07):

- target ~94–97% accuracy at ~25% coverage at the 90s mark
- **accuracy WITH edge** (beat the price paid, not just track the market)
- fire-ASAP; 120s soft / 180s hard deadline (later overridden — see §3)
- BTC 5m only (15m markets excluded)
- new interchangeable version; v6 stays untouched
- required: an honest ≥8/10 confidence statement before trust

The reframe that made it tractable: this is **selective prediction with abstention**, not
predict-every-bar. The project's own record shows forced calls are the accuracy killer
(v6 lean fallback ≈ 60% vs strong tier 87–96%). v7 fires only when signals converge and
**abstains** otherwise.

## 2. In-sample measurement (live logs)

Frontier measured by firing the market's side when `|poly_mid − 0.5| ≥ t` (264 usable 5m
logs, settle = Polymarket-verified):

| fire mark | t=0.20 | t=0.25 | t=0.30 | t=0.35 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 60s in | 82.7% @28% | 86% @16% | 93.3% @11% | 94.4% @7% |
| 90s in | 88.6% @46% | 91.3% @34% | 94.3% @26% | 92.3% @14% |

Key facts found here:
- `poly_mid` (Polymarket's own price) is the dominant early predictor; AND-ing in our
  flow/book/whale signals slightly *hurts* (the price already contains them).
- Winning band (real tick-loop replay, 278 live logs): **poly_mid ∈ [0.82, 0.93] +
  cushion-agree + dwell 3, window 45–90s in** → **95.6% acc, +10.5pp edge, 24.5%
  coverage, median fire 69s**.

## 3. The 90s hard deadline (deliberate spec override)

The spec allowed fires to 180s. Measured edge decays hard by window: 45–90s
**+10.5pp** → 90–120s **+2.3pp** → 120–180s **+0.9pp** (accuracy 95.6% → 88.7% → 87.6%).
Bars that only reach the band late are slow coin-flips the market has already repriced.
So the engine's hard deadline is **90s**, abstaining after — more accurate AND earlier
than the permitted window. Flagged to and accepted by the operator at decision time.

## 4. The out-of-sample collapse — the load-bearing finding of v7

Worker exported the full BigQuery `bin` history → `v7/analysis/bq_eval.jsonl`
(**709 bars, 59.2h continuous 24/7, Jul 5–7**, no session-selection bias; no truncation,
raw pulls under the 500k-row cap).

**The in-sample result did not survive: 95.6% / +10.5pp → 81.7% / −2.8pp.**

Five measured diagnoses (all tested, none guessed):
1. **Not hour-of-day** — BQ bars score ~85% both inside and outside live-session hours.
2. **Not data-source quality** — on 105 bars covered by both sources, fired sides agreed 25/25.
3. **Bar-selection luck** — live sessions happened to run during cleaner stretches; the
   709 continuous bars are reality.
4. **Misses are invisible at fire time** — price, vol, cushion, flow, whales,
   perp-divergence, absorption, trailing regime: hits and misses statistically identical
   on every feature. Genuine post-fire reversals; **no veto exists in these inputs**.
5. **The market is calibrated** — at 45–90s, Polymarket's price ≈ its accuracy in every
   bucket. **Edge from public data ≈ 0.** A 95%-at-60s signal from this data would already
   be priced — and measurably is.

Structural ceiling: **~8% of bars settle within $10** (oracle coin-flips no predictor can
call), capping anything near ~96%.

Confidence ledger, as demanded: original spec (94–97% + edge + 25% cov, 24/7) — **2/10,
not achievable from these inputs**. v7 as recalibrated below — **8/10** (883 deduped
bars, held-out-validated, known cascade-day risk).

## 7. First vs second signal (operator question of record)

- **EARLY CALL: no second signal exists, by design.** One immutable latch per bar or
  abstention. Basis: re-evaluation churn measured at 45.5% (dir→MIXED→same-dir bars) —
  the second look at the same conclusion added nothing and stuck to losers.
- **Lean-stream tag (inherited byte-identical from v6): the 2nd call beats the 1st**
  (140-bar set 66%→77–78%; re-verified v5.3 64%/78%, v6 59%/74%). The lift concentrates
  in direction *changes* (66.7% when flipping away from a wrong first call);
  reaffirmations gained nothing. Practical read: trust the badge; treat the opening lean
  tag as the least reliable read on the page.
